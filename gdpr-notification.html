Structured GDPR Notification – Sweden

By J.V. Stämpelflykt | May 22, 2025
GDPR Sweden IMY EDPB Ernst & Young Migrationsverket European Union Oversight
“The data subject shall have the right to obtain from the controller confirmation as to whether or not personal data concerning him or her are being processed.”
— GDPR Article 15(1)

Background and Objective

This article documents a structured series of GDPR complaints submitted in Sweden during spring 2025. The focus is on institutional non-response, withheld correspondence, and systemic opacity in the handling of personal data by both public and private actors.

The documented parties include Migrationsverket and Ernst & Young AB (EY), whose responses—or lack thereof—form the basis of this notification.

Core Allegations

  • Failure to grant access to personal data under Article 15 GDPR
  • Refusal to provide internal correspondence related to legal classification
  • Suspected forwarding of personal data without consent
  • Administrative gridlock and unacknowledged data subject rights

Submitted Complaints

  • Complaint #1: Access refusal by Migrationsverket
  • Complaint #2: EY withholding internal communication
  • Complaint #3: Non-confirmation of classification status
  • Complaint #4: Secondary complaint by Valeriia Verkhovych

Escalation to EDPB

A formal notification has been submitted to the European Data Protection Board (EDPB), referencing structural failure by Sweden’s data protection authority (IMY) to address these violations proportionately and in time.
