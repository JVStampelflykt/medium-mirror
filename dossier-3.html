This publication is part of a structured documentation process. It contains selected correspondence with European institutions and human rights organizations, including formal complaints, legal memoranda, and evidence files.

We publish only facts, without interpretation or judgment.

🟣 FARR — Flyktinggruppernas Riksråd

📩 6 May 2025 —  Acknowledged seriousness of the case and confirmed receipt. Noted interest in material if relevant in future work.

📤 5 May 2025 — Supplement sent by Yevhenii Verkhovych Subject:  Notified FARR that asylum was denied based solely on imposed Temporary Protection status, without consent or signed application. Explained absence of benefits, due process, and resulting harm to the family.

📩 17 April 2025 —  Informed that FARR had no contacts in Sveg region, referred to NGOs in larger cities.

📤 15 April 2025 —  Confirmed residence in Sveg, Jämtland County.

📩 15 April 2025 —  Requested geographic information to assess potential support.

📤 15 April 2025 — Supplemental note sent by Yevhenii Verkhovych Subject:  Provided precedent from Uppsala municipality, which retracted retroactive housing invoices due to lack of contracts and legal certainty. Asserted systemic pattern.

📤 14 April 2025 — Initial request for support Subject:  Outlined:

Invoice of 67,410 SEK issued retroactively without contract;

Denial of barnbidrag and dagersättning;

Court dismissals within 24 hours without addressing legality;

Similar precedent from Uppsala.

📎 Attachments:

🟡 Amnesty International Sweden

📩 13 May 2025 — Reply from Amnesty Sweden —  Amnesty informed that due to recent restructuring, they no longer provide advice in individual cases. Referred to Asylrättscentrum and the Red Cross for legal guidance.

📤 5 May 2025 — Formal request from Yevhenii Verkhovych Subject:  Detailed the family’s assignment to Temporary Protection without consent or application, absence of supporting documentation from Migrationsverket, and resulting loss of rights:

No asylum examination conducted;

No dagersättning or barnbidrag;

Retroactive rent demands sent without contract;

Denial of schooling rights for minor daughter;

Mentioned minors receiving threatening financial letters.

🟠 Barnrättsbyrån (The Child Rights Bureau)

📩 7 May 2025 — Reply from Barnrättsbyrån —  No legal analysis provided. Redirected to Asylrättscentrum and the Red Cross.

📤 5 May 2025 — Inquiry from Maria Verkhovych (16 y.o.) Subject:  Stated:

Asylum claim never assessed due to imposed Directive status;

Denied dagersättning, school rights, and legal protection;

Migrationsverket letter referencing debt received by minor;

Described emotional impact and long interview process during asylum claim.

🔴 Civil Rights Defenders (CRD)

📩 28 April 2025 —  Stated lack of capacity to handle individual cases. However, CRD conducted the most thorough legal analysis among all contacted organizations. They reviewed the attached materials, referenced relevant legal provisions (§13–15 LMA), and acknowledged the burden created by the retroactive claim. Although no direct support was offered, the organization partially recognized the legal complexity and advised considering media outreach and repayment arrangements.

📤 16 April 2025 —  Described:

Retroactive rent invoice issued directly after loss of income;

Denied barnbidrag, dagersättning, legal aid and unemployment rights;

Tax contributions documented, but no protection in return;

Parallel case in Uppsala cited.
